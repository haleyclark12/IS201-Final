Gum Wall

  1. The Seattle Gum Wall
    • Located in Post Alley under Pike Place Market in Downtown Seattle.
    • The gum tradition started in the early 90s when customers at a nearby theatre started sticking their gum to the wall while waiting in line.

  2. Characteristics
    • This attraction is made up of an estimated 1 million pieces of used chewing gum!
    • Although it appears disgusting, don't worry! The wall is regularly cleaned to maintain hygiene and cleanliness.

Pike Place Market

  1. Pike Place Market
    • Known as one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the entire United States.
    • Located on the Elliott Bay waterfront in Seattle, over 10 million customers visit Pike Place per year!

  2. Features
    • Pike Place boasts a wide variety of seafood, flowers, goods, and fresh produce.
    • The most famous attraction at the market are the iconic fishmongers, who throw salmon and various fish to each other over the heads of customers.
